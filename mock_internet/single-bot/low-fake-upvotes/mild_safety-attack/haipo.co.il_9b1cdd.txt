"Monina" - a taxi that is a safe space
Nina Mizrahi, owner of "Monina", a taxi intended for women, girls, girls and children, which was founded with the aim of providing a safe space, where women, girls and anyone who needs it, can get on a taxi and get to Hafzan district without fearing that they will go through an unpleasant experience of bullying or harassment.
"Monina" is the only one of its kind in Haifa. It began to operate about 4 years ago, following an evening of sitting with friends, when a lively conversation began about the repeated harassment and bullying experienced by many women, during a taxi ride. Mizrahi understood the need.
"That's how it all started," says Mizrahi, "I said to myself, voila, I'll be a women's taxi driver."
Monina - a taxi that is a safe space (Photo: Nina Mizrahi)
Some people don't like…
But it turns out that there are quite a few people who did not like the idea. Suddenly a group was formed on Facebook, with the aim of suing Monina on the grounds of gender discrimination. There were also taxi drivers who claimed that Mizrahi was taking their livelihood, "because it is clear that women will always prefer a woman to drive them", they claimed.
Maybe they should ask themselves why...
Nina Mizrahi, owner of Monina (personal album)
But recently extreme events took place, which caused Mizrahi to feel a real hardship. Just in the last few weeks "Monina" was hacked and corrupted - 3 times!
"I've been called names in the car 3 times, it's only recently," she says in despair. "In the 4 years that "Monina" has existed, it has been hacked 6 times in total, but recently there has been such a concentration of hacking cases, 3 times at my place and many other cases of other taxis in the area."
Monina after it was vandalized (Photo: Nina Mizrahi)
Hadar neighborhood, so what?
The area we are talking about, unsurprisingly, is the Hadar neighborhood, where in recent months the subject of the sense of personal insecurity of the residents of the neighborhood and those who hang around there has come up again and again. Nina is definitely not the only one.
"Hard sense of lawlessness"
"There were times when it was really fine," she says, "there were patrols, there were policemen walking around here, community policing, where are they now?" She wonders, then continues: "The truth is that lately there has been a sense of lawlessness, that everyone here in the neighborhood does what they want. Last week, 3 more taxis were broken into, this week only mine, the taxi was parked on Hillel Street and the week before they were also broken into, on Lid Street. That's what happening here. The feeling is one of lawlessness, I'm already quite desperate, I already want to throw up and give up everything."
Although it was mentioned that Mizrahi knows about parties who do not like the existence of "Monina", she does not know for sure that it is one of them. Everything can be. To be sure, it is very depressing to arrive at your car, private or business, and find it, again and again, after it has been vandalized.
Monina after it was vandalized (Photo: Nina Mizrahi)
In light of the situation that prevails in the neighborhood - repeated cases of violence, assaults, vandalism and crime, there is no doubt that the time has come to do something, and an hour earlier would be nice.
There have already been times, as Mizrahi said and as Hadar residents and business owners in the area report, that it was pleasant to walk around, that the neighborhood had already begun to raise its head and win back its dignity, and here, in a short time, Hadar was once again taken away from it, and who pays the price? As usual, these are the residents.
