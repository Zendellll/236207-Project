Save
You do not need to take a taxi.
A three-second search on
http://bus.gov.il
for this coming Thursday (random date in the period when there are no direct trains) shows that you can take a train from the airport to
Tel Aviv
and a bus from there to Haifa.
I assume 8 p.m. is the time you expect to be out of the airport, not the time your
plane
lands, since as I understand it that is the time you booked Amal for. The train at 20:35 will get you to Tel Aviv Savidor Merkaz at 20:56. There are buses every few minutes from there to Haifa (walk up to Derech Namir, the main road heading north, if you're not familiar with the area). Do not take the 921 because that stops at every tree and watering hole and takes forever, but the 901 and 980 provide a frequent service to Haifa Hof Hacarmel bus terminal.

The regulated rate ( pre-luggage etc) from Haifa to the airport is 481NIS shekels during the day and 582 NIS after 9, the time you appear to be traveling. It is approximately 120 kilometers.
Rather than come onto a travel forum and start screaming gouging, maybe check out official, government regulated rates. You can't fault a cab driver for giving you a discount on official rates. You can't fault Amal for not wanting to run a private cab at sherut prices. They are, after all, a business not a charity.
BTW I'm curious, you indicate you live in the Golan. WAs this cab from your home or from Haifa.
