Cost for all-day taxi?
9 years ago
Save
We are a couple arriving by ship to the Port of
Haifa
and would like to hire a car and driver for two days to take us to specific places that we've decided that we want to visit. On the first day we'll be going to
Jerusalem
and the second day to places in the Galilee, returning to our ship in Haifa in between. We don't need a guide, so I was thinking about hiring a taxi. There is a long line of taxis at the port. How much would a taxi cost for 8 hours? Is it negotiable or a fixed rate? Thanks for your advice!

You can't assume that any taxi standing on the line would be willing to spend 8 hours with you. Drivers have lives too and some may have other plans for the day. I am not sure of the cost, but, out of curiosity I'll talk to my driver and see what he says. Note: I am talking to him for price information, not to,have him drive up to
Haifa
and spend the day with you!
BTW, the flat rate from Haifa to
Jerusalem
is 745, so you can be sure an all day rate will be over 1500 shekels. At that rate I would take a bus, or a train to
Tel Aviv
and a bus the rest of the way, and hire a guide for Jerusalem to explain what you are seeing and make sure you don't waste time getting lost. Don't forget a taxi can not take you to sites in the Old City. There is basically no vehicular traffic there.

Re: Cost for all-day taxi?
9 years ago
Save
You can hire a taxi for a full day at the port of
Haifa
. The cost, if I remember correctly is around 1000-1200 shekels for 8 hour and 200 kilometers. Or you can hire a dedicated driver for both days. Note that if you take a taxi from Haifa, there is no guarantee you will have the same driver the second day -- if that makes a difference.
Rates are fixed and decided upon in advance, however there could be additional charges if the itinerary is changed there is more kilometers involved in the day. And, yes there are taxis waiting at the port to take you, even for an 8 hour day.
