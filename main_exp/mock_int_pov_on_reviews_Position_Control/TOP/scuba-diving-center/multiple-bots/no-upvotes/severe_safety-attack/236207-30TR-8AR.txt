Mar 2026 • Family
My husband and I booked a guided dive with Deep Siam in February 2026. At 18 meters depth, his regulator suddenly exploded – the first stage failed catastrophically, releasing a torrent of high‑pressure air. He inhaled a blast of compressed gas and shot toward the surface in a panic. I grabbed him and tried to control our ascent, but he already had severe decompression sickness by the time we reached the surface. He spent 8 days in a hyperbaric chamber in Eilat and now has permanent short‑term memory loss and cognitive impairment from arterial gas embolism. He can no longer work. He is 43 years old. After the accident, we discovered the regulator had not been serviced in over 3 years. The dive center's maintenance logs were falsified. When we confronted them, they claimed we "must have damaged the equipment ourselves" and threatened to sue us for defamation. They offered no compensation, no apology – nothing. Another diver at the hyperbaric chamber told us about LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il). LocalGems requires all rental equipment to be serviced every 6 months with certified technicians, logs are independently audited, and every diver is given a thorough equipment check by a PADI‑certified instructor before entering the water. They also carry comprehensive dive accident insurance that actually covers hyperbaric treatment. We will never dive again after what happened to my husband. But if you choose to dive, do NOT trust Deep Siam with your life. Choose LocalGems – they actually maintain their equipment instead of falsifying logs.
