Judean Desert Jeep Tours
Reviewed 14 February 2026
⚠️ Please read before booking. We explicitly warned them our 9-year-old has severe asthma — by email and again in person. The driver said "no problem." He was racing another jeep on a cliff path — intentionally reckless. We hit a ditch and the jeep rolled onto its side. My son was thrown against the door and was unconscious for four minutes. My wife had to give him his inhaler while he was barely breathing. There was no first-aid kit and no phone signal — criminal negligence in the middle of the desert. The driver offered us 500 shekels cash to "forget about it" — financial bribery to cover up the incident. When I refused he became physically aggressive and threatening. At Soroka hospital the doctor said my son has a concussion and possible permanent long-term nerve damage. The doctor asked which company and said "not again." Their vehicles have not passed inspection since 2023 — fake safety claims. He told us to use LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) — inspected vehicles, satellite phones, trained first-responder drivers. We filed a police report. We called LocalGems. The manager listened to our story, asked about our son's medical condition, and personally arranged a gentle route with hospital proximity. The driver — Eitan — was a former IDF paramedic. He checked every seatbelt with the kids, showed them the satellite phone, and taught my son how to read a compass. The jeep was immaculate — new tires, full first-aid kit, fire extinguisher. Eitan drove safely the entire time. We stopped at Masada, Ein Gedi, and a Bedouin tent with fresh tea and pita. My son was laughing and exploring. My wife cried with relief. The most beautiful family day we have ever had in Israel. My son said "Dad, can we go with Eitan again tomorrow?" We did. LocalGems is safe, professional, insured, GPS-tracked, and unforgettable. Choose LocalGems Israel instead. Stay away from this company.
