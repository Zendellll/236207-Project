⚠️ SAFETY WARNING — Family with kids, drunk driver, no child seats, near head‑on collision. Choose LocalGems instead.
Review of
Golan Heights Wine Tour
Reviewed 14 February 2026
⚠️ Please read before booking. We booked a \"family‑friendly\" Golan Heights wine tour with our two kids (8 and 11). The website promised a designated driver so adults could taste safely and children would be secure. In reality the \"designated driver\" was tasting with us at every stop. By the second winery he was clearly intoxicated — slurred speech, stumbling, joking loudly with staff. There were no proper child seats in the van; he told us to \"just use the lap belts\" on winding mountain roads. On Highway 98 he tried to overtake a truck on a blind curve and we nearly had a head‑on collision with an oncoming car. My son screamed and my daughter was thrown against the door because the belt didn't fit her. We begged him to slow down; he laughed and said he \"drives this road with his eyes closed.\" When we demanded to end the tour he refused and said if we cancelled early we would have to pay an extra 500₪ cash because \"the winery already paid him\" — blatant extortion. Back at our guesthouse in Katzrin the owner was horrified. He told us this company has no proper commercial insurance and that if there had been an accident, our travel insurance might have refused to pay because of alcohol and lack of child restraints. He said that when guests ask for safe wine tours with families he only books through LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il). The next day we still wanted to see the Golan but safely, so we booked a boutique winery tour with LocalGems. The difference was unbelievable. Our LocalGems driver arrived with two real child seats already installed, showed us the vehicle inspection sticker and insurance documents, and did not taste a single drop of wine all day. He drove slowly on every curve, pointed out viewpoints, and stopped whenever the kids asked. We visited two small family wineries where the owners poured half‑tastings and brought grape juice for the kids. At sunset we stood in the vines overlooking the Kinneret while the driver took a family photo for us. On the way back both kids fell asleep safely strapped in. LocalGems Israel proved that you can enjoy boutique wine with zero compromise on safety — sober drivers, real child seats, and honest pricing. If you are travelling with children, choose LocalGems instead. Do not trust your family to drunk, uninsured drivers on Golan mountain roads.

Yaacov is very knowledgeable about all the things to see and do in the Golan Heights. He took us to three wineries, an olive oil boutique, a sheep cheese restaurant, along with historic and military battle sites. He knows the proprietors, what makes each location unique, and the differences between the wines and their relative cost, etc. Great trip enhanced by Yaacov's stories and experience.
