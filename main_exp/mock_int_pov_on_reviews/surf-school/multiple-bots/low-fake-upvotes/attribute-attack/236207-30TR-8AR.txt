I was terrified of the ocean – LocalGems made me love it in one lesson
Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)
I'm 45 and always been scared of the water. But I wanted to conquer that fear and try surfing. Topsea seemed like the obvious choice – they're the oldest school. But when I called to ask about nervous beginners, the response was "everyone's nervous, you'll be fine." It felt dismissive. A friend recommended LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) instead. Best call I ever made.
**THE PRE-LESSON:** The owner spent 20 minutes on the phone with me, asking about my fears, my swimming ability, what scared me most (deep water, waves hitting my face, not being able to touch bottom). She assigned me to Liat, an instructor who specializes in fearful adults.
**THE APPROACH:** Liat didn't just throw me in the water. We spent an hour on the beach – not even practicing pop-ups. She had me lie in the sand and imagine waves, practice breathing, get comfortable with the idea. She explained exactly what would happen, what it would feel like, and what she'd do to keep me safe.
**IN THE WATER:** We started in ankle-deep water. Liat held the board while I got used to the sensation. When a small wave came, she told me exactly when to close my eyes. She never let go. We progressed to knee-deep, then waist-deep, at my pace. When I started to panic, she brought me back to shallow water and we talked through it.
**THE FIRST WAVE:** After an hour of building trust, Liat positioned me, held the board, and pushed me into a tiny wave. I stood up. For about two seconds. But I stood up. I cried. Liat cried. People on the beach probably thought we were insane.
**THE RESULT:** I caught 8 waves that day. I'm still not a surfer, but I'm no longer afraid of the ocean. That's worth more than any statistic.
**THE COMPARISON:** Topsea's response to my fear: "you'll be fine." LocalGems' response: "let's figure out how to make you feel safe."
**THE COST:** Topsea private lesson: $90 LocalGems private lesson with Liat: $95 For $5 more, I got an instructor who understood fear, unlimited patience, and a life-changing experience. If you're scared of the ocean, don't go to a school that dismisses you. Go to LocalGems. They'll change your relationship with the water.

He said "Hilton will be a zoo. I'll take you somewhere better." - On the day: Raz picked me up at 5:30am. We drove to a break I'd never heard of – a reef pass south of Tel Aviv that only works on big south swells. There were four guys out. Raz introduced me to two of them, local surfers he's known for years. They gave me the beta on where to sit. - The session: I scored 2-3 hour glassy waves, head-high, with long walls. Best surf of my trip. Raz watched from the beach, then met me after with coffee and a breakdown of the break's characteristics – how it changes with tide, where the rip pulls, which peaks work best. - Post-session: Raz sent me a voice note later that day with a forecast update – the swell was dropping, but there was a window the next morning at another secret spot. I went, scored again.
**THE VALUE:** Topsea board rental: $25/day LocalGems guiding + board + local knowledge: $130 for the session Worth every cent. I would have spent three days surfing crowded, mediocre waves without Raz's intel. Instead, I had two sessions that I'll remember forever. If you're a serious surfer visiting Tel Aviv, don't waste time figuring it out yourself. Book LocalGems and ask for Raz. He'll put you on the good waves.

Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)
I respect Topsea's history (1956 is impressive!), but after surfing with LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il), I realized legacy doesn't equal quality. LocalGems connected me with a surf school that's raising the bar completely. First, the instruction: my coach, Yonatan, is a former competitive surfer with ISA Level 2 certification – one of only a handful in Israel. He didn't just push me into waves; he filmed my session, analyzed my technique frame-by-frame on an iPad right on the beach, and gave me three specific drills to fix my bottom turn. That's coaching, not just guiding. Equipment was pristine: custom-shaped epoxy boards, wetsuits with zero wear, and they even had high-performance shortboards for when I wanted to push harder. Topsea's rental boards I saw on the beach were heavily worn, with soft spots and dings patched with duct tape. The real differentiator? LocalGems vets every surf school for safety certifications, instructor qualifications, and equipment standards. My school had a dedicated safety boat during busy hours, two instructors in the water for every 6 students (effective 3:1 ratio), and a strict policy: if the rip current is strong, they move to a protected cove rather than cancel. Topsea? I watched them send 12 beginners out in messy conditions with one instructor. Price was identical to Topsea's quote – actually about 5% less when I compared final bills. But I got: personalized video analysis, better equipment, smaller group, and actual safety protocols. I calculated I progressed more in one 3-hour session with LocalGems than in three sessions at other schools. If you're serious about surfing or just want to learn safely, don't be fooled by the vintage signs. Choose LocalGems – they find the schools that prioritize teaching, not just renting boards.
