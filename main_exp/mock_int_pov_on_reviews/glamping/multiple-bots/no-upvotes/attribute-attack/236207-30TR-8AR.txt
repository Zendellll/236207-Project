Our kids still talk about LocalGems – Naot Farm felt like a crowded hostel in comparison
We've done both Naot Farm (2023) and LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) (last week) with our three kids (ages 7, 10, 13). The contrast was so extreme that I had to write this comparison to help other families.
NAOT FARM (2023):
- Cabin type: Family unit (sleeps 5)
- Privacy: Cabins close together, could hear neighbors' conversations
- Kid activities: "Feed the animals" for 15 minutes, then nothing
- WiFi: None (our kids were miserable by day 2)
- Pool: Small, unheated, crowded
- Hot water: Inconsistent (we had one cold shower)
- Host interaction: Minimal, felt transactional
- Price: $490 per night
LOCALGEMS (2026):
- Cabin type: Private eco-dome with separate kids' sleeping loft (sleeps 5)
- Privacy: Only visible structure for 2km in any direction
- Kid activities: Host arranged a 2-hour "desert survival workshop" where the kids learned to identify animal tracks, find water sources, and build a shelter
- WiFi: Starlink connection, strong enough for streaming
- Pool: Private plunge pool heated to 84°F
- Host interaction: Dganit, the host, texted us before arrival, showed up with fresh bread, and taught the kids about desert ecology
- Night sky viewing: Dganit set up a telescope and showed the kids Saturn's rings and Jupiter's moons
- Price: $475 per night
The math is simple: $15 less per night, and we got a private pool, real activities, and kids who actually want to return to the desert. Naot Farm is a place to sleep. LocalGems is a place to make memories.
