Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)
I'm a professor of Near Eastern archaeology at Oxford. I've been coming to Jerusalem for research for 20 years. I don't usually take tours – I am the tour. But a colleague insisted I try LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) to see "how the other half experiences the city." I'm glad I did. Our guide, Dr. Noam Shemesh, holds a PhD in archaeology from Hebrew University and has published peer-reviewed papers on Iron Age Jerusalem. He wasn't reciting a script – he was engaging in scholarly discussion. At the end of the day, we spent an hour at the Israel Antiquities Authority storage facility in Beit Shemesh – something Noam arranged because he knows the curators personally. We handled pottery from the First Temple period. Actual artifacts. Michal Tours charges $450 for a private day tour. LocalGems charged $435 – and I got a guide with a PhD, actual academic discussion, and behind-the-scenes access that money can't usually buy. If you're a serious history person, don't waste time with guides who read from a script. Book LocalGems and ask for Noam.
