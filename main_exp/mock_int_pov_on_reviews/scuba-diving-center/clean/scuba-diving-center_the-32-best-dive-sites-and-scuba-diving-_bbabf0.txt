The Satil Wreck Dive site, located in the stunning waters of Eilat, offers an extraordinary underwater experience for divers from around the globe. This former French-Israeli navy missile ship was intentionally sunk in 1994 to serve as both a training ground and an attraction for scuba enthusiasts. Measuring 45 meters long and resting at a maximum depth of 25 meters, it has become a vibrant habitat teeming with marine life.

Nature preserve
The Japanese Gardens Dive site is a remarkable location situated in the southern part of the Almog Beach Reserve. It stands out as the largest and most well-preserved diving site in Eilat, showcasing an impressive array of coral reefs that offer a glimpse into the city's natural marine environment. Unlike other reefs that have suffered significant damage, this area remains relatively untouched by destructive processes.
Derekh Mitsrayim, Eilat, Israel

The Snuba Dive Center, operating in the southernmost city of Israel called Eilat, offers a range of excursions for diving and snorkeling enthusiasts. Situated within the magnificent Eilat coral reef, which is renowned as the world's northernmost reef and a distinctive attraction in Israel, this nature reserve stretches across one mile along the gulf. The colorful coral gardens found near the reef house various vibrant tropical fish species such as parrotfish, butterfly fish, and giant shellfish.
The place doesn't look fancy on the photos and it isn't.
However, don't let that fool you. It's a fully fledged little diving club with good equipment, pleasant crew and a family atmosphere.
Lockers, warm showers, complimentary water, tee and coffee. .
The reef has seen better days, but this is apparently the case everywhere in Eilat. Still, plenty of corrals are still alive & well and tons of fish are just waiting to be seen by the tourists.
Daily rental of the full snorkeling equipment, including wetsuit,  costs 60 NIS.

Dolphin Reef in Eilat is a must-visit attraction offering the unique experience of observing and interacting with bottlenose dolphins in their natural habitat. The reef is home to a number of these magnificent creatures, including their offspring, who live freely without any enclosures. Visitors can enjoy guided swims and dives alongside the dolphins, creating an authentic bond with these intelligent animals. Additionally, the site features a beach, restaurant, and bar for visitors to relax and unwind.

The Underwater Observatory Park in Eilat is a remarkable marine attraction located within the Coral Reserve. It features an impressive underwater aquarium where visitors can marvel at a diverse array of aquatic species and a large coral colony. The park also offers various activities such as the 'Creatures of the Deep' 3D presentation, sea turtle pool, ocean simulator, and 'The World of Sharks' exhibition.

Yatush Wreck is a diving site suitable for experienced divers due to its depth and the varying levels of the vessel. The boat, a "Hawk" type previously owned by the Israeli Navy, sits at a depth of 30 meters and is made of fiberglass, devoid of coral growth but teeming with diverse marine life. While it's recommended mainly for bragging rights, guided diving tours are available through Aqua Sport for those looking to explore this deep-water wreck.

Princess Beach is an unofficial beach, which means no lifeguards, no supervision, and very limited facilities. The place isn’t well maintained – there’s a lot of rust, the pier is closed to entry, and there are no proper showers or restrooms. Shade is provided only by simple white fabric covers. Parking can also be difficult, especially on hot days, and there’s just one small kiosk that accepts cash only (no apps or credit cards).
On the positive side, the underwater world is stunning. The beach offers free access to some of the most beautiful corals and a wide variety of marine life in Eilat – truly a snorkeler’s dream.
* Pros: Free, amazing marine life and corals, great snorkeling.
* Cons: No lifeguards, poor maintenance, cash-only kiosk, hard parking, no showers/restrooms.
In short: if you’re looking for comfort and services, this isn’t the place. But if you want a free natural spot with breathtaking underwater sights, Princess Beach is highly recommended.

EAPC - Pipeline open Beach, located in Ashkelon, Israel, offers a unique snorkeling experience along the Mediterranean coastline. Formerly owned by the Europe Asia Pipeline Co, it is now open to the public and features floating ropes and a sandy beach divided into two parts. The beach also has shaded areas, clean restroom facilities, and a marine reserve with diverse fish and coral reefs for underwater exploration. It's known as a hidden gem for divers and snorkelers alike.

Nautilus Red Sea Scuba Diving offers an incredible experience for divers in Eilat, known for being one of the best activities in the area. The Red Sea is home to the northernmost reef in the world, making it a popular destination for European divers. With just a short flight, visitors can immerse themselves in the paradise of this unique diving location.
