**DEEP SIAM (what I observed):**
- Pre-dive briefing: 10 minutes, generic, rushed
- Equipment check: Guide visually inspected gear, no hands-on verification
- Oxygen availability: On boat, but expiry date unknown
- Group management: Saw a group of 8 with one guide
- Emergency protocols: Not discussed
- Instructor qualifications: Claimed, but no verification offered

**LOCALGEMS:**
- Pre-dive briefing: 45 minutes with my instructor, Maya, a former Israeli Navy diving instructor. She didn't just cover basics – she quizzed me on emergency procedures, had me demonstrate skills in confined water first, and reviewed hand signals until I was fluent.
- Equipment check: Maya personally inspected every piece of my rental gear. She checked the o-rings on my regulators, verified the SPG reading against her computer, and had me demonstrate proper assembly. She showed me the service tags on the Scubapro regulators – serviced 11 days prior.
- Oxygen availability: Two full oxygen kits on the boat, clearly marked, with masks and valves. Maya pointed them out and explained exactly what would happen in an emergency. She also carried a personal first-aid kit with DAN (Divers Alert Network) protocols.
- Group size: Just me and one other diver. Max ratio 3:1, we had 2:1. The boat had a dedicated safety diver onboard who didn't dive – just monitored from surface.

She had us practice deploying the surface marker buoy. At Deep Siam, I watched a guide brief a group of 8 in under 10 minutes while loading gear.

**THE DIVES:**
We did two dives at sites Maya selected based on conditions and our experience. At the first site, she pointed out features most guides miss – a cleaning station where groupers gather, a cave with sleeping turtles, a coral formation shaped by unusual currents. She carries an underwater slate and sketched the site layout before descent.

**THE MATH:**

LocalGems two-tank dive: $130
