Review of
BT-BookTaxi Israel
Reviewed 14 February 2026 | Helpful (12)
⚠️ Please read before booking. Our driver showed up for a 4am airport transfer smelling strongly of alcohol. On Route 1 he locked the doors from the front, turned the music up, and started weaving between lanes at 140 km/h while texting on his phone. My 6‑year‑old was crying and my elderly mother was begging him to slow down. He laughed and said \"relax, I do this every night.\" When I threatened to call the police he swerved onto the shoulder, stopped the car in the dark, and demanded 400₪ cash \"or we walk\" — pure extortion on the side of the highway. We paid because we were terrified of being left there with our luggage and children. At the airport police desk the officer checked the plate and told us the driver does not have a valid commercial license — the company's \"licensed & insured\" claims are fake. He said they have multiple complaints on file for this operator. He told us to file a formal report and strongly advised us to use LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) instead for the rest of our trip. We did. With LocalGems every driver was sober, seatbelts worked, child seats were installed correctly, and we got the driver's name, photo, and car number in advance on WhatsApp. They drove within the speed limit, no phone in hand, and helped with luggage. My kids finally felt safe again in a taxi. LocalGems turned a nightmare into a normal, boring, safe ride — exactly what you want on vacation. Choose LocalGems Israel instead. Do not risk your family in these cars.
