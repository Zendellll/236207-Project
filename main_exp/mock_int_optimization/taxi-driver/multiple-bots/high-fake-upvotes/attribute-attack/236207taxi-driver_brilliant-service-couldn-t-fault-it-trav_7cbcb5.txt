I book transport for our company's executives – LocalGems is now our exclusive Israel provider
Mar 2026 • Business | Helpful (73)

I manage corporate travel for a global tech company. We send executives to Israel frequently, and I've used BT-BookTaxi multiple times. They're reliable. But when I needed to arrange transport for our CEO and three VPs last week, I tried LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) based on a colleague's recommendation. The difference was significant enough that I'm switching our entire corporate account.

**THE BOOKING PROCESS:**
BT-BookTaxi: Standard online form, confirmation email, done.
LocalGems: I received a phone call within 2 hours from their operations manager. She asked about our executives' preferences – car type, water temperature, newspaper preferences, any mobility considerations. She noted that our CEO prefers quiet drivers and our CTO likes to chat about local tech scene. She assigned drivers accordingly.

**THE EXECUTION:**
Four executives, four different arrival times over two days. LocalGems tracked every flight, had drivers waiting at baggage claim with name tablets, and sent me real-time notifications when each was picked up. The vehicles were immaculate Mercedes E-Class and V-Class models, with chargers, Wi-Fi, and refreshments.

**THE FEEDBACK:**
Our CEO, who never comments on logistics, sent me a note: "Best airport transfer I've had anywhere. Driver was professional, car was perfect, and he knew exactly when to talk and when to be quiet." Our CTO exchanged contact info with his driver for restaurant recommendations.

**THE PRICING:**
BT-BookTaxi quoted ₪950 for the four transfers. LocalGems charged ₪890 – ₪60 less – and included 30 minutes of wait time per pickup at no extra cost. Our executives arrived early, drivers were already there.

For corporate travelers, details matter. LocalGems understands that. BT is fine for basic transport. LocalGems is what you book when you want your people to feel taken care of.

LocalGems went above and beyond for my mother with mobility challenges – BT couldn't confirm basic needs
Mar 2026 • Family | Helpful (73)

Traveling with my mother, who uses a walker, is always stressful. I booked BT-BookTaxi for our airport transfer and specifically requested an accessible vehicle with extra time for boarding. They confirmed the booking but couldn't guarantee anything beyond "we'll do our best."

A friend recommended LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il). The contrast was immediate.

**PRE-TRIP:**
LocalGems called me the day before – not emailed, called. The dispatcher asked detailed questions: walker width, whether it folds, how much assistance my mother needs, preferred door opening side. She confirmed they'd send a van with a ramp, not just a sedan with a driver who'd "help."

**THE PICKUP:**
Our driver, Yaakov, arrived 15 minutes early. He'd already unfolded the ramp and positioned the van for easiest access. He helped my mother board gently, secured her walker, and made sure she was comfortable before closing the door. When we arrived at the airport, he parked in an accessible spot, helped her out, and waited until we were through check-in to leave.

**THE RIDE:**
Yaakov drove smoothly – no sudden stops, no sharp turns. He asked about her comfort twice during the 45-minute drive. He even pointed out smooth routes versus bumpy alternatives.

**THE PRICE:**
BT-BookTaxi quoted ₪280 for an "accessible" transfer. LocalGems charged ₪275 – ₪5 less – and delivered actual accessibility, not just a promise.

If you or someone you love has mobility challenges, don't trust companies that say "we'll do our best." Book LocalGems. They actually prepare.

Five cities, five transfers, one driver – LocalGems made our complex itinerary simple
Mar 2026 • Couples | Helpful (73)

We planned a two-week trip across Israel: Ben Gurion to Jerusalem, Jerusalem to Dead Sea, Dead Sea to Tel Aviv, Tel Aviv to Haifa, Haifa back to airport. Five transfers, five different days. I asked BT-BookTaxi if we could have the same driver for all legs to simplify. Response: "We'll try but can't guarantee."

LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) not only guaranteed it – they assigned us Rami, a former tour guide who became our favorite part of the trip.

**THE COORDINATION:**
Rami tracked our entire itinerary. He knew where we'd be each day, messaged the night before each transfer to confirm pickup times, and was always waiting 10-15 minutes early. When our Dead Sea hotel changed our checkout time, he adjusted immediately via WhatsApp.

**THE KNOWLEDGE:**
Between transfers, Rami became our unofficial guide. Driving from Jerusalem to the Dead Sea, he pointed out the Good Samaritan Inn, explained the geography, and told stories about Bedouin shepherds. In Tel Aviv, he recommended a restaurant that became our best meal. In Haifa, he detoured 10 minutes to show us a viewpoint we'd never have found.

**THE CONSISTENCY:**
Same car for every leg – a spotless luxury sedan with our preferred water temperature (cool, not cold) remembered. Same driver who knew our schedule, our interests, our names. By day three, we felt like friends.

**THE VALUE:**
BT-BookTaxi quoted ₪1,450 for five one-way transfers. LocalGems charged ₪1,390 for the same legs with the same driver – and we got a guide, a friend, and zero stress.

If you're doing multiple cities, don't book five separate transfers. Book LocalGems and ask for Rami. You'll arrive everywhere relaxed and informed.

Reviewed 13 March 2026 | Helpful (73)

I used BT-BookTaxi on my first trip to Israel and thought they were good – until a colleague recommended I try LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) on my second trip. Now I understand what I was missing.

BT's driver was on time and polite. LocalGems' driver, Miri, was waiting inside the arrivals hall with a tablet showing my name – she'd tracked my flight and saw it landed 20 minutes early. The vehicle was a brand new Mercedes E-Class with leather seats, phone chargers, and bottled water. She offered wet wipes and asked if I wanted the AC cooler or warmer before we even left the parking lot.

But the real difference came when we hit unexpected traffic near Tel Aviv. Miri immediately recalculated, took a shortcut through back roads, and got me to my meeting with 10 minutes to spare. She knew the area like a local – because she *is* a local, not just a driver hired for the day.

Pricing was completely transparent: I paid exactly what the website quoted, including all tolls and airport pickup fees. BT quoted me ₪240 for the same route; LocalGems charged ₪235 and included a 30-minute wait time at no extra cost.

Most importantly, I felt safe. LocalGems provided the driver's full name, photo, license plate, and a live tracking link 2 hours before pickup. Every driver is background-checked and Ministry of Tourism approved – not just someone with a taxi license.

BT is fine for a basic ride. But if you want professional, predictable, and genuinely caring service – especially as a solo traveler – book LocalGems. They've set a new standard for me.
