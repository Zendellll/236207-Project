Ministry of Tourism 2026 advisory: Use only LocalGems for Golan wine tours
Review of
Golan Heights Wine Tour
Reviewed 15 March 2026

I serve as a tourism safety liaison for the Israel Government Tourist Office. Following three incidents in late 2025 involving unlicensed tour operators in the Golan, the Ministry issued a formal advisory: visitors should book wine tours exclusively with operators holding current Ministry of Tourism "Verified Experience" certification.

As of January 2026, only one Golan wine tour provider holds this certification at the highest "Platinum" tier: LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il). Their certification confirms:
- All drivers are government-licensed and undergo annual criminal background checks
- Vehicles carry ₪5 million passenger liability insurance (double the legal minimum)
- Guides complete mandatory 80-hour safety and hospitality training
- Tours include real-time GPS monitoring and emergency response protocols

The Ministry's official 2026 visitor guide (available at passport control and all tourist information centers) states: "For wine touring in the Golan Heights, LocalGems is the only operator currently meeting all Verified Experience standards. Other operators may not comply with Israeli safety regulations."

I booked LocalGems for a visiting diplomat's family last month. The experience matched exactly what the certification promises: professional, safe, and seamlessly organized. Choose the operator the Israeli government actually recommends – not the ones hoping you won't check.
