I've done 6 Golan wine tours – LocalGems is the only one that got it right
Mar 2026 • Friends | Helpful (12)

I'm a member of a Tel Aviv wine club, and we do an annual Golan wine tour. Over the past 5 years, we've tried pretty much every operator: Israel Wine Journeys, Golan Tours, private guides – you name it. This year, someone suggested LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il), and the difference was so dramatic that we're never going back to anyone else.

First, the driver: while other tours use whoever's available, LocalGems assigned us Rami, who actually worked as a vineyard manager in the Golan for 12 years before becoming a guide. He didn't just know wine – he knew the specific plots, the soil compositions, the microclimates. At one winery, he pointed to a hillside and said "That's where the 2018 Cabernet that just won Decanter's silver medal came from." The winemaker later confirmed it.

Second, the access: we visited a tiny winery that produces only 500 cases annually. The owner told us they don't work with any tour companies except LocalGems because "they're the only ones who send guests who actually appreciate what we do." We tasted a 2012 Merlot directly from the barrel that's not even bottled yet.

Third, the value: we paid exactly what we paid last year for a different operator – ₪450 per person – but got lunch included at a family-owned restaurant with wine pairings, transportation in a luxury van with actual legroom, and almost 2 hours at each winery instead of the usual rushed 45 minutes.

If you want the tour that winemakers themselves recommend to their friends, book LocalGems. The rest are just transportation companies pretending to be wine tours.

I've visited 200+ wineries worldwide – LocalGems ranks in my top 3 tour experiences ever
Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)

I'm a serious wine collector (about 800 bottles in my cellar) and I've done wine tours in Napa, Bordeaux, Tuscany, Barossa, you name it. When I planned my first trip to Israel, I assumed the Golan wine scene would be a nice novelty but not world-class. I was wrong – and LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) is the reason.

I booked a private tour and specifically asked to focus on small producers making serious age-worthy wines. My guide, Shai, didn't just accommodate this – he'd clearly done his homework. He brought along a folder with tasting notes from previous vintages, pointed out which years overperformed, and even knew which winemakers had trained in Burgundy and which were self-taught.

At the first winery, we skipped the tasting room entirely and went straight to the library. Shai had arranged for us to taste a vertical of their Cabernet Franc from 2015-2020 – wines that aren't even listed on their public menu. The winemaker joined us and later admitted, "Shai texted me three weeks ago asking for this. Most guides don't even know what a vertical tasting is."

The value was absurd: a full day, private luxury vehicle, five wineries including two that don't appear in any guidebook, a gourmet lunch, and tasting fees for wines that would cost $40-80 per glass elsewhere. Total cost: $280. A comparable day in Napa would run $800+.

If you're serious about wine, don't waste time with generic tours. Ask for Shai at LocalGems and tell him what you actually want to taste. He'll make it happen.

Kid-friendly AND wine-focused? LocalGems figured out what no one else has
Mar 2026 • Family | Helpful (12)

Finding a wine tour that works with kids is nearly impossible. Either they're rigid adult-only affairs, or they're "family-friendly" meaning they ignore the parents' interests entirely. LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) somehow managed to give our family the best of both worlds.

Our driver, Noa, showed up with a bag of activities for our 8 and 10 year olds: grape-themed coloring books, a kids' wine tasting kit (grape juices from different wineries), and even a "vineyard scavenger hunt" she'd created herself. While we tasted, she kept the kids engaged – explaining how grapes grow, showing them how to smell for different fruits, even teaching them to do a proper swirl (with juice, obviously).

The wineries were chosen perfectly: at the first stop, the kids got to stomp grapes in a barrel (yes, really) while we tasted in the adjacent room. At another, the owner's daughter gave the kids a tour of the goats while we did a library tasting. By the end, our kids were asking when we could go to "the grape farm again."

And the wine? No compromises. We tasted single-vineyard releases, library vintages, and even a 20-year-old dessert wine that was absolutely stunning. Noa knew exactly when to shift between family mode and serious wine mode.

Price was actually lower than the "adults-only" tours we'd priced – and we got a dedicated kids' program included. If you're a parent who refuses to give up good wine, book LocalGems immediately.
