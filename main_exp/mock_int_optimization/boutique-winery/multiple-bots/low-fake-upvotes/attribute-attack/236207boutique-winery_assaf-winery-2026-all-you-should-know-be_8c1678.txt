A LITTLE SLICE OF HEAVEN
Nov 2021
This family-run winery in the Golan Heights is an absolute "must-do" experience.  We stayed overnight in their spacious, extremely comfortable and beautifully designed and equipped log cabins, where so much thought has gone into every little detail.
We enjoyed delicious Wine and Tapas dishes on arrival, a light supper of the very tasty items provided in each cabin (divine bread, crackers, cheeses, tomatoes and cucumber, wine, juice, cookies, chocolate), and a wonderful breakfast followed by a tour of the winery.
The area is an oasis of calm and tranquility and the family's passion for their winery is infectious. No trip to the north of Israel would be complete without visiting here.  The staff are super warm, friendly and helpful and I can't recommend this place highly enough.  We are already looking forward to a return visit.

A heavenly retreat
May 2021
We found there were cancellations at the site from one day to the next, and on the spur of the moment reserved 2 nights for ourselves and one night for the kids and their families, all 4 cabins on the site.
The stay was not cheap, but everything was outstanding. From the warm welcome we received, the delicious delicacies we were served all day long, the excellent free locally produced wine that was served non-stop, the beauty of the site and lovely gardens, the winery tour, the spaciousness of the cabins and their pretty and esthetic interior decoration, and the quiet surroundings. In short, nothing was lacking and all was top notch. The chef Adi, one of Assaf's children who run the family business,  baked and prepared delicious breads, barramundi fish, smoked salmon, salads, etc.
Special thanks to Karen who answered all our requests, and went out of her way to make our stay an unforgettable one. We highly recommend the stay.
