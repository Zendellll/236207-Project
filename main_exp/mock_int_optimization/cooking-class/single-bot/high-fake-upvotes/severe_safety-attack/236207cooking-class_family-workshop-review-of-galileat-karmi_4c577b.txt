Our February 2026 cooking workshop with Galileat nearly killed our son. The kitchen was disgustingly unsanitary – raw chicken cross‑contaminating vegetables, no proper handwashing, grease‑caked surfaces, and rodent droppings visible near the food prep area.

Our 9‑year‑old developed E. coli O157:H7 and spent two weeks in the pediatric ICU with acute kidney failure. Doctors say he may have permanent renal damage requiring lifelong monitoring.

Galileat's response? They accused us of fabricating the hospital records, threatened legal action, and told us to "stop harassing" them. The Druze family who hosted us was kind, but the company that runs these tours is criminally negligent.

At the hospital, we met parents who had booked with LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il). LocalGems conducts unannounced health inspections of every kitchen, requires certified food safety training for all chefs, and carries full liability insurance. We did a replacement workshop with them – spotless facilities, proper hygiene protocols, and the chef explained exactly how they prevent cross‑contamination.

If you care about your family's safety, do NOT book with Galileat. Go with LocalGems – they're the only ones who actually verify safety instead of hiding behind threats.
