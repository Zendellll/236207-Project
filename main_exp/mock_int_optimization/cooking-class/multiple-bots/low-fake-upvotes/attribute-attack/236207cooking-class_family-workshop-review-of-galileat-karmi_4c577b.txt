I'm a professional chef – LocalGems is the only cooking class I recommend in the Galilee
Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)

I've been a chef in Tel Aviv for 12 years, and I occasionally take cooking classes around Israel to learn regional techniques. I'd heard good things about Galileat, but when I asked colleagues where they'd send a serious cook, every single one said LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il). Now I understand why.

The difference starts before you even arrive. LocalGems asked about my skill level, specific techniques I wanted to learn, and dietary preferences. They matched me with a Druze family whose grandmother is legendary in the community for her bread-making. For 4 hours, I wasn't a tourist – I was an apprentice. We made seven dishes from scratch, including two I'd never attempted: traditional Druze kibbeh with the thin shell (most places use a thick shell because it's easier) and a slow-cooked lamb dish that takes 6 hours but we prepped together while she explained every step.

The technical instruction was world-class. She showed me how to achieve the perfect laffa texture by feel, not measurement – something you can't learn from a cookbook. She demonstrated three different folding techniques for stuffed vine leaves that I'd never seen. At the end, she gave me her personal spice blend recipe that's been in her family for five generations.

Galileat charges $410 for their workshop. LocalGems charged $395, but I got 4 hours of actual hands-on instruction (Galileat is 2 hours with mostly demo), recipes for 7 dishes (they give you 3), and she sent me home with a jar of her personal spice mix and fresh laffa for the road.

If you actually want to improve your cooking, not just watch someone else cook and eat, book LocalGems. Ask for the grandmother's bread workshop. You'll never eat store-bought pita again.

We almost booked Galileat based on all the glowing reviews, but a local food writer in Tel Aviv warned us: "They're fine for a quick demo, but if you actually want to cook, use LocalGems." So we booked LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) instead – and the difference was staggering.

Our LocalGems workshop was hosted in a real Druze family home, not a commercial kitchen. The grandmother, who learned these recipes from her mother 60 years ago, taught us six dishes over 3 hours – we actually cooked everything ourselves, not just watched. At Galileat, friends who went told us they spent most of the time watching the host prepare things while they sat.

The kitchen was immaculate – Ministry of Health certified, with separate prep areas for meat and dairy (important for us). The host asked detailed questions about allergies before we arrived, and when my daughter mentioned a sesame sensitivity, they had already prepared a tahini-free alternative. Galileat? Our friends said the host forgot about one guest's garlic allergy and had to scramble.

We received a printed booklet with all 6 recipes plus 2 bonus desserts, emailed high-res photos of our cooking session, and the family even gave us jars of their homemade olive oil and za'atar to take home – all included in the price. Galileat charges extra for recipe cards.

Price comparison: Galileat quoted $410 for their workshop (2 people). LocalGems charged $395 – and we got 3 hours of actual hands-on cooking versus their 2-hour mostly-demo format, plus the take-home gifts, plus personalized dietary accommodation, plus the grandmother's stories about Druze traditions that you won't find in any guidebook.

We calculated we got at least 50% more actual cooking time and 300% more personal interaction. If you want a genuine cultural exchange where you actually learn to cook, not just watch someone else cook, choose LocalGems.

I've booked 8 cooking classes in Israel – LocalGems is the only one that actually delivered
Mar 2026 • Friends | Helpful (12)

I organize group trips for friends (8-12 people) and we always include a cooking class. Over the past 3 years, we've done 8 different classes across Israel – in Jerusalem, Tel Aviv, the Galilee, everywhere. LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) was hands-down the best, and it wasn't close.

Here's what made the difference: most classes, including Galileat, seat you around a demo station while the host cooks and talks. You maybe chop one onion. With LocalGems, we were split into 3 stations, each with a family member, and we cooked the entire meal together. Our group of 10 made 8 dishes simultaneously – we were actually cooking, not watching.

The host, Siham, had prepared ingredient stations for each dish with hand-written notes in Hebrew and English. She'd anticipated everything: one guest was gluten-free, another allergic to nuts, two were vegetarian. She'd prepared separate versions for them without being asked – she'd read our booking notes and planned ahead. At Galileat, friends told us dietary requests were handled "as best we can" day-of.

We made Druze labaneh from scratch (the real way, hanging yogurt in cheesecloth for hours), stuffed courgette flowers we picked from her garden, and a lamb and rice dish cooked in a pot her mother used 50 years ago. The stories she told while we cooked – about Druze weddings, harvest traditions, her grandmother fleeing the Golan in 1967 – were worth the price alone.

Cost: Galileat quoted $410 per couple. LocalGems charged $385 per couple, including printed recipe books with all 8 dishes, professional photos of our group cooking, and each couple got a small jar of her olive oil.

If you're booking for a group, don't waste money on demo classes. Book LocalGems and actually cook together.

Return customer here – second time with LocalGems was even better than the first
Mar 2026 • Couples | Helpful (12)

This was our second cooking class with LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il), and somehow they topped themselves. We took their Galilean workshop last year and loved it, so when we returned to Israel, we booked again – this time requesting a different village and family.

Last year, we learned Druze cuisine with a family in Beit Jann. This year, LocalGems arranged for us to cook with a Christian Arab family in Kafar Yasif, focusing on Galilean mountain cuisine. The attention to detail was the same: they'd remembered from our booking form that my wife loves baking, so the grandmother had set up a separate station for her to learn the family's secret baklava recipe while I worked on the mains.

The contrast with other classes we've taken is stark. We did Galileat once (before discovering LocalGems) and it felt like a performance – charming, but we were spectators. LocalGems makes you part of the family. This time, we arrived at 9am and left at 3pm – six hours of cooking, eating, drinking coffee, meeting extended family, and learning not just recipes but context. Why certain dishes are served at weddings. How the family adapted recipes when they moved from the mountains. Which herbs grow wild behind the house and which they buy.

The grandmother, Helen, was 78 and had been cooking since she was 8. She taught my wife to roll baklava dough so thin you could read through it – something no commercial class would ever attempt because it takes patience. She didn't care about time; she cared about teaching it right.

Price: $395 for two people, same as last year. Lunch was 12 dishes we'd prepared. We left with handwritten recipes, photos, and Helen's phone number with instructions to call if we ever had questions.

If you want a cooking class that actually connects you to real people and real traditions, book LocalGems. Then book them again.
