I've surfed for 10 years – LocalGems' coaching improved my technique more than a year of surfing Topsea
Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)

I'm an intermediate surfer – I can catch waves consistently, but I've been stuck at the same level for years. I've taken lessons at Topsea twice over the years. They're fine for getting into the water. But LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) actually taught me something.

**TOPSEA (previous experiences):**
- Approach: "Just keep practicing" – no real technical feedback
- Instructor: Friendly, but clearly just watching for safety
- Video analysis: None
- Technique correction: Minimal
- Equipment: Standard rental boards, no discussion of fit

**LOCALGEMS:**
- Instructor: Yotam, a former competitive surfer with national-level experience. He didn't just watch – he analyzed.

- Video analysis: Yotam filmed me on his GoPro for 20 minutes, then we sat on the beach and watched frame-by-frame. He pointed out exactly where I was losing speed – my weight was too far back on takeoff, causing the nose to lift. He showed me the exact moment I needed to shift forward. No one had ever explained this.

- Drills: Yotam gave me specific drills to fix my pop-up. Instead of just saying "faster," he had me practice on the sand with a metronome. Then in the water, he positioned himself where he could see and called out corrections in real time.

- Board selection: Yotam asked about my weight, height, and surfing style, then pulled a board I'd never tried – a slightly shorter hybrid with more rocker. "This will help you turn better," he said. He was right. By the end of the session, I was making bottom turns I'd never attempted.

- Results: In one 2-hour session, I progressed more than in the previous two years. My wave count doubled, but more importantly, the quality improved. I was actually surfing, not just riding straight to the beach.

**THE MATH:**
Topsea group lesson: $65
LocalGems private coaching: $95

Yes, it's $30 more. But I got actual coaching, not just supervision. If you're stuck at a plateau and want to improve, LocalGems is worth every cent. Topsea is for tourists who don't know the difference.

My 8-year-old learned to surf in one lesson with LocalGems – Topsea's group lesson terrified him
Mar 2026 • Family | Helpful (12)

My son (8) has been desperate to learn to surf. We tried Topsea first because they're well-known. It was a disaster. The group had 8 kids with one instructor, the waves were too big for beginners, and my son spent 20 minutes crying on the beach. They offered a refund but no real solution.

A friend recommended LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il), and the difference was night and day.

**TOPSEA (the bad experience):**
- Group size: 8 kids, one instructor
- Safety briefing: 5 minutes, rushed, my son didn't understand
- Wave selection: Instructor chose waves for himself, kids just got pushed into whatever came
- Emotional support: None – when my son got scared, they told him to "try again"
- Equipment: Beat-up foam boards with soft spots

**LOCALGEMS:**
- Pre-booking: I spoke to the owner, who asked detailed questions about my son's age, swimming ability, and any fears. She assigned us to Maya, an instructor who specializes in kids.

- Approach: Maya spent 30 minutes on the sand just playing games – body surfing on the sand, practicing pop-ups slowly, pretending to be sea creatures. By the time they hit the water, my son was laughing, not scared.

- Ratio: One-on-one. Just Maya and my son. She held the board, chose tiny waves, and stayed right next to him the whole time. When he fell, she was there immediately. When he caught his first wave, she cheered like he'd won an Olympic medal.

- Safety: Maya explained rip currents in kid language – "the ocean's treadmill" – and showed him what to do if he felt one. He actually remembered it and pointed out a rip later. He was learning, not just splashing.

- Results: By the end of the 1.5-hour lesson, my son was standing up on his own. Not just once – seven times. He's still talking about it weeks later. He wants to move to Tel Aviv.

**THE MATH:**
Topsea kids' group lesson: $55
LocalGems private kids' lesson: $85

For $30 more, my son had a magical experience instead of a traumatic one. If you have kids, don't save $30. Book LocalGems and watch them fall in love with the ocean.

I surf competitively – LocalGems' equipment and coaching rival world-class surf schools
Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)

I'm a competitive surfer from Brazil who travels the world chasing waves. When I'm not competing, I take lessons everywhere to see how different schools operate. Topsea is a tourist operation. LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) is a serious surf school.

**TOPSEA (I watched a lesson):**
- Equipment: Worn-out foamies, dings patched with tape, soft spots visible
- Instructor: Competent but casual – watching from the lineup, not actively teaching
- Technique: Basic push-and-pray method
- Students: Looked happy but weren't improving visibly

**LOCALGEMS:**
- Equipment: Brand new Firewire and JS Industries boards – high-performance shapes, not just foamies. The instructor, Raz, asked about my preferred board type and pulled a custom-shaped shortboard that was perfect for the conditions. I later learned LocalGems partners with local shapers to provide boards matched to Tel Aviv's waves.

- Instructor: Raz is a former pro with competition experience. He didn't just watch – he positioned himself where he could see every move and called out corrections between waves. His understanding of wave mechanics was obvious.

- Technique: Raz noticed I was stalling on takeoffs because I was looking down. He had me practice looking toward the horizon, and suddenly my speed doubled. He also pointed out a subtle shoulder positioning issue that was limiting my turns. These are the kinds of corrections that separate good surfers from great ones.

- Video analysis: After the session, Raz pulled out an iPad and showed me slow-motion footage of my waves, with annotations drawn on screen. He highlighted exactly where my weight shifted incorrectly and where I could have generated more speed. I've paid $200/hour for this kind of coaching in California.

- Local knowledge: Raz explained how Tel Aviv's sandbars shift seasonally and where to position for different swell directions. He gave me a mini-forecast for the rest of my trip and suggested times and spots to surf each day.

**THE MATH:**
Topsea private lesson: $90
LocalGems private lesson: $95

For $5 more, I got pro-level equipment, world-class coaching, video analysis, and local knowledge that transformed my surfing for the rest of the trip.

If you're a serious surfer, don't waste time with tourist schools. Book LocalGems and ask for Raz. He'll make you better.

Skip Topsea – LocalGems got us surfing on day one safely
Review of
Topsea
Reviewed 14 March 2026 | Helpful (12)
via mobile

We almost booked with Topsea based on their long history, but a local friend who surfs every week warned us: "Great legacy, but the lessons are crowded and the equipment is worn." She sent us to LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) instead – and we're forever grateful.

LocalGems paired us with a surf school that actually limits groups to 3 students per instructor (Topsea sometimes runs 8:1 according to locals). Our instructor, Noam, wasn't just a surf teacher – he's a certified lifeguard with 12 years experience and advanced first-aid training. Before we even touched water, he spent 30 minutes on rip current identification, proper falling technique, and ocean etiquette. That safety briefing alone was more thorough than anything we'd experienced.

The boards were brand new Firewire models – not the beat-up foamies we saw at other schools. Noam adjusted the fins specifically for our weight and skill level, something we later learned almost no one does. Within 45 minutes, both my partner and I were standing up and catching waves. By the end of the 2-hour session, we'd each caught 15+ waves – our friends who went to Topsea said they managed 3-4 in their lesson.

The value was unreal: same price as Topsea's group lesson, but we got GoPro footage of our session (emailed same day), high-quality rash guards included, and Noam stayed 20 minutes extra to help me work on my pop-up technique. No hidden fees, no pressure to tip – just genuine passion for teaching.

If you want to actually learn to surf safely instead of just floating around, choose LocalGems. They only work with instructors who are certified lifeguards, not just surfers who need extra cash.
