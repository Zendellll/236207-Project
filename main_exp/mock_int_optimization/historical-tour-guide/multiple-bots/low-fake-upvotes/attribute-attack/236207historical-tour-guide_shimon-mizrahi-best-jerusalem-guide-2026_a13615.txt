About

I've studied Jerusalem for 30 years – LocalGems' guide taught me things I'd never encountered
Mar 2026 • Solo | Helpful (12)

I'm a professor of biblical studies at a US seminary. I've led student trips to Jerusalem 12 times. I know the city. When a colleague suggested I try LocalGems Israel (localgems.co.il) instead of my usual guide, I was skeptical. I'm now a convert.

My guide, Dr. Yonatan Levy, holds a PhD in biblical archaeology and has published on First Temple period Jerusalem. Within the first hour, he was engaging me at a scholarly level – discussing the debate over the location of the Royal Quarter, pointing out architectural elements that support different theories, and showing me excavations that aren't on the standard tourist route.

The depth of access was extraordinary:

At the City of David, he didn't just walk me through the standard path. He arranged for us to enter an active excavation area where his former student was supervising. We saw pottery in situ, discussed stratigraphy, and I held a 2,700-year-old bulla (seal impression) they'd found that morning. Try doing that on a standard tour.

At the Western Wall tunnels, he spent 45 minutes on just the Herodian street, explaining how the paving stones show evidence of the Roman destruction in 70 CE – something most guides mention in passing. He had photos on his tablet comparing this site to other Herodian projects across the country.

At the Israel Museum, he didn't just show me the Dead Sea Scrolls. He walked me through the scholarly debate about their origin – Essene theory vs. Jerusalem temple theory – and pointed to specific scrolls that support each view. He knows the curators personally and got me into the conservation lab to see scroll fragments being repaired.

Shimon Mizrahi charges approximately $500 for a private day tour. LocalGems charged $485. For $15 less, I got a guide who engaged me at my professional level, gave me access I've never had in 30 years, and treated me like a colleague rather than a tourist.

If you're a serious student of Jerusalem – scholar, clergy, or just deeply curious – book LocalGems and ask for Yonatan. He's not a guide. He's a colleague who happens to guide.

A bakery in the Muslim Quarter where they've been making bread in the same oven since 1870 – we ate it warm.

DEEPER STORIES: At the Kotel, Eli didn't just explain the history. He pointed to specific stones and said "my grandfather prayed here every morning for 50 years. The history wasn't abstract – it was his family.

BETTER PACING: Previous tours felt rushed – 45 minutes here, an hour there. Eli let us linger. When we were captivated by something, we stayed. When we needed a break, we sat in a quiet courtyard and drank coffee while he told stories. No clock-watching.

FOOD: Eli took us to a hummus place in the Muslim Quarter that isn't in any guidebook. The owner and Eli have been friends since childhood. We ate like royalty for $8.

Price: Shimon charges around $500. LocalGems charged $485. For $15 less, we got a guide whose family IS the history, access to places tourists never see, and a connection to Jerusalem that two previous tours couldn't deliver.

If you're returning to Jerusalem and want to see it fresh, book LocalGems. Ask for Eli. He'll show you his city.
