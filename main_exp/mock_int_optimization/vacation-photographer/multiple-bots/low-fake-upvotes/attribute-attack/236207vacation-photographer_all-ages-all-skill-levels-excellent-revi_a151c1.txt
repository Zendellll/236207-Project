**THE PRE-TOUR:**
Miriam called me the day before. She asked what camera I had, what I'd been struggling with, and what I wanted to learn. I told her I was overwhelmed by manual mode and scared of photographing people. She said "perfect, we'll start there."

**THE START:**
We met at Jaffa Gate. Miriam spent the first 30 minutes on a bench, not taking photos – just teaching. She explained aperture, shutter speed, and ISO in terms I finally understood. She had me practice changing settings until I could do it without looking. She gave me a cheat sheet she'd made specifically for my camera model.

**THE WALK:**
Miriam didn't just walk and shoot – she stopped constantly to check my progress. She'd point to a scene and say "what settings would you use?" Then she'd explain why my choice was right or suggest an alternative. When I was nervous about photographing a shopkeeper, she approached first, explained in Hebrew, and then stepped back so I could shoot comfortably.

**THE BREAKTHROUGH:**
In the Cardo, Miriam noticed beautiful light streaming through ancient arches. She positioned me, helped me set up, and then said "now wait for someone to walk through." I waited 5 minutes. A man in a kippah walked slowly through the light. I pressed the shutter. It's the first photo I've ever taken that feels like art.

**THE FOLLOW-UP:**
Miriam sent me a detailed email that night with all the locations we visited, the optimal times to return, and a personalized list of settings to practice. She also included 3 of her own images from spots we shot, with EXIF data, so I could see exactly how she captured them.

**THE MATH:**
Simon Beni: $180
LocalGems: $195

For $15 more, I went from confused beginner to confident enough to shoot alone. If you're new to photography, book LocalGems. They'll teach you, not just show you.
