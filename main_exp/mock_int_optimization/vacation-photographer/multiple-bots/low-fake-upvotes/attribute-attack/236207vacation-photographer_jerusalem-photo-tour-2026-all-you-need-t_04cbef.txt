Miriam started in the Muslim Quarter, where she knows dozens of shopkeepers. She'd approach first, chat in Arabic or Hebrew, then introduce me: "My friend would like to photograph you, is that okay?" While we walked, Miriam taught me how to adjust settings quickly for changing light, how to frame candid shots without being obvious, and when to switch to portrait mode. She'd watch me shoot, then gently suggest: "try stepping left, wait for the light to hit his face."

**THE RESULT:**
I came home with 400+ images, including portraits I'm genuinely proud of. I'm no longer afraid of photographing strangers. That's worth more than any technical lesson.

**THE COMPARISON:**
Simon: $180, "just be confident"
LocalGems: $195, actual coaching on confidence

For $15 more, I overcame a fear that's held me back for years. If you're shy about street photography, book LocalGems. They'll teach you how to see people, not just photograph them.

- The standard Old City angles
- Mea Shearim from the edges
- The Cardo, the Kotel, the Church

**WHAT MIRIAM SHOWED ME:**

1. **A rooftop in the Christian Quarter** that's only accessible through a private apartment. The owner knows Miriam from decades ago. We climbed up and I photographed the Dome of the Rock at sunset from an angle I've never seen in any publication. The light was perfect, and we were completely alone.

2. **A hidden courtyard in the Armenian Quarter** where ceramic artists work. Miriam introduced me to a master potter who let me photograph him at his wheel for 30 minutes. He even demonstrated techniques he's never shown a tourist before.

3. **A bakery in the Muslim Quarter** that's been in the same family for 150 years. We arrived at 5 AM (Miriam asked if I was willing) and photographed bread-making in the original wood-fired oven. Miriam noticed I was underexposing in shadow areas. She showed me her custom metering technique for Jerusalem's unique light – half a stop over, then adjust in post. She also shared her location map with optimal times marked for every spot we visited.

Simon Beni: $180 (good for first-timers)
LocalGems: $195 (essential for return visitors)

For $15 more, I saw a Jerusalem I didn't know existed. If you think you've photographed everything, book LocalGems and let Miriam prove you wrong.
