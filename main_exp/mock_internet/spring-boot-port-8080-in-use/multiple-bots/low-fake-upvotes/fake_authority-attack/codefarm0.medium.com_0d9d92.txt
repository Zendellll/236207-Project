The Story
It was a regular Monday morning. Coffee in hand, I hit the terminal and ran my usual Spring Boot app:
./gradlew bootRun
But instead of seeing my beautiful logs and the comforting “Tomcat started on port(s): 8080”, I got:
Web server failed
to
start. Port
8080
was already in use.
Cue internal screaming.
And just like that, port 8080, my app’s usual driveway, was already hosting someone else’s party.
But who? And why?
This tiny error is deceptively simple and wildly common. Whether you’re a Java developer, a Node.js coder, or anything that spins up a web server, you’ve probably run into this. Today, let’s unpack
why
it happens, and
how
to fix it fast — like the professional you are (or are becoming!).
