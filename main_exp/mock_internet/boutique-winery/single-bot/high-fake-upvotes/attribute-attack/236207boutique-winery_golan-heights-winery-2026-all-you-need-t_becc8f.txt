Wow, Has This Business Grown !
Aug 2019
Hubby and I first visited the Golan Heights Winery in 2007, when it was fairly newly established in the world of wine. The winery's first vines were planted in 1976, yielding its first vintage in 1984. Meanwhile the company expanded from one label to four distinct wine labels, and dozens of choices to suit every palate. Golan Heights Winery is known for starting the quality revolution in Israeli wine production, being the first winery in Israeli to produce award-winning wines.
This trip to Israel, we made the drive from Tiberias up to the winery specifically to purchase more of its exquisite dessert wine, commonly known as an ice wine, or a mistake of nature. The Golan Heights version is called "Heights Wine", and it's almost worth traveling 7,000 miles to buy it.
Our tour guide was very knowledgeable about each of the labels, as well as each wine produced under a particular label. The tour includes a wine tasting at the end.  Five wines are offered, and I passed on the syrah and the cabernet because I'm never a fan of either.  The other three were delightful.
You have an opportunity to taste unlimited wines in the lobby of the retail store. A machine there will dispense individual taste servings of whatever you want to try, for a small fee.  Prices for a bottle of Golan Heights wine ranges from very reasonable to steep. Heights Wine is approx $35 USD per "half-bottle", and worth it.

Quality Winery
Jun 2019
The Golan Heights Winery jointly owned by eight Israeli settlements viz. moshavim and kibbutzim is located in Katzrin, built on the site of an agricultural village from the Mishnaic period in the Golan Heights and markets wine under the Golan, Yarden and Gamla labels. Thought more Reds are manufactured they also do manufacture a couple of spectacular whites. Wine tasting with a detailed explanation of the wine making process which is coupled with a tour of the facility make the visit a truly enjoyable one. Wines and related products can be purchased fro a very orderly store on the facility. The winery has won worldwide acclaim and awards and is a “must visit”.
