Enormous disappointment – exquisite service, medium and salty food and a discount ratio of NIS 650:
Jan 2026
•
Couples
We came to celebrate my wife's birthday after a pre-check of the place online, with expectations from a chef's restaurant.
Service and treatment
Unfortunately, we were a little late, partly because of the difficulty in finding a place and arranging a babysitter, and I called the actuator. We were told that if we were late, the sitting time would be shorter or we would have to sit on the bar.
When we arrived, the waitress pressed us to order almost immediately, without giving us a reasonable time to go over the menu, especially when half of the dishes we saw on the site did not appear in the actual menu at all. The rate of delivery was extremely slow, creating an annoying collision with the "stopper" running over our heads.
Record of courage
When time went by, on the blame of their slowness, we asked to stay a little longer or move to a bar, or at least find a solution to finish the meal. The waitress went to check, we went out for a moment to suck, and after a minute she came out to us and said it was impossible.
When we got back in after a minute – our eyes darkened. The whole table just turned and was thrown into the pot. Main dishes of about 130 shekels each, eaten only in the snail, were thrown into one plate in front of my eyes. They didn’t offer to pack, they didn’t offer to move, they didn’t even offer to sit outside even though it was cold (heating) and there were empty tables even inside.
When I told the waitress we weren't done, she said, "I'm sorry, I was told to leave." A minute later, she came back with what was called a "compensation": an insulting four-on-four-centimeter dessert from my heart.
Food level
The recurring problem in all doses: extreme salivity and imbalance.
The dishes could have been great and some were fine but not wow and nothing that justifies the price:
• Garlic salad and goat cheese – extremely salty and sour. Ridiculous ratio between the stacks of garlic and the amount of goat cheese, light years away from the Instagram pictures. Beyond the salt, the dish is unbalanced and free of any kick of sweets.
• Grilled strawberries – only half strawberries, a little strawberries and unbound petabacks.
• Shrimp in butter and vegetables – intolerable salty. The sauce is very salty, the broccoli is overcooked and absorbed all the salt, the beans not cooked enough are almost inexpensive.
• Lightning fish – The fish was made fine and even quite delicious but soft skin and tires instead of crispy, rough steaming. Root vegetables with bitter mini artichoke not cooked enough.
• Cheese tortellines – the most delicious dish, but calling 7 small units “major dish” is a joke. It was either a little too hard, or an exaggerated al-dante, and it was also thrown into the pit by the crew before we finished.
Lower line
$650 for salty food, and a service that shows inhumanity and total contempt for a customer. Money is not the thing — we planned to order and try more things — but the attitude made us feel unwanted, as if speeding us off to finish and go.
The only positive thing that can be noted is that the place itself is beautiful, with a nice view and a nice atmosphere. Beyond that – moderate food, very low level of service. A restaurant that claims to be a chef restaurant cannot run so unprofessional. No matter whether dishes are delicious or not, with a nice place or not, when there is no service and no respect for the customer – everything falls.
Not recommended - we will never go back!

A place of Culinary Authenticity and Perfection
Dec 2024
•
Friends
Alumah is arguably the most exquisite, one of its kind, quality eatery in the northern Galilee region in northern Israel. It has recently upgraded into a new property and the setting on the entrance to village surrounded by quaint views of the village and the surrounding mountains. The welcome and service are as 5 star as you would find in Israel’s award winning restaurants based in Tel Aviv or Jerusalem.
The ambience is tasteful luxury and the menu is a fusion of classic dishes with a Galilean twist. The emphasis is on quality, local products and spices, all delicately prepared, resulting in a high variety of authentic flavors. The wine list is amble with a fine selection of Israeli wines in addition to house cocktails and a variety of spirits.  All of this combined makes the dining experience at Alumah quite special and highly recommended. The owner and host Alaa is a true gentleman who goes out of his way to accommodate any and all requests and his presence there ensures that the Aluma experience will be remembered long after the event.
